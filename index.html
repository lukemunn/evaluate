Build Your Own Online Safety Evaluation Scheme

Smaller organisations should select at least 2 indicators at each scale, larger agencies with more resources should select at least 3.

  • Individual (Micro)

  • Institutional (Meso)

  • Sociocultural (Macro)

  • Indicators

    • Scale of School Aggression

      "aggressive behaviors toward peers in the school context in the past 12 months" Consults participants directly Can be subjective and prone to bias Smith and Smith 2016

    • Hotline / Helpline Calls

      number of calls to a hotline or helpline during a period, pre and post intervention Keeping Children Safe Online

    • "3 Ways" Test

      ability of child to list three ways to protect themselves online, pre and post intervention Keeping Children Safe Online

    • Teacher / Parent Interview

      interview conducted with parent or teacher regarding ability of a child to protect themselves online Keeping Children Safe Online

    • Digital Narrative

      A story generated by the child about the most significant change that resulted from the programme, collected through a digital form or input mechanism Can synthesise complex data into a digestible form, with beginning / middle / end Can obscure themes (evident in a thematic analysis); involves small number and potentially biased case studies River of Life 2017

    • Cyberbullying Language

      Identifies aggressive or abusive language using machine learning models or other technical means. Measuring the amount or intensity of this language over time can provide one indicator of programme's effectiveness. Automatic and able to be measured frequently Highly technical to setup, privacy issues, lack of sociocultural context ACM Archive of Papers 2016-2020

    • Platform Reports

      Measure the number of cyberbullying or grooming instances reported across a particular platform, pre and post intervention, or even longer if possible. Concrete statistic that can help "round out" other measures Highly ambiguous, higher numbers could be due to more visible or accessible reporting mechanism, rather than increase in incidents Facebook, Twitter, Riot Games 2014-2020

    • Campaign-Style Statistics

      Measures views, visits, number of messages shared, duration of session, and other statistics typically captured when deploying online marketing campaigns. Concrete statistic that can demonstrate engagement and "round out" other measures Tends to measure the popularity of the programme rather than providing indication of any behavioral change Spears et al. 2016 "Safe and Well Online" project
